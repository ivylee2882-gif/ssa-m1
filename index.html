Overview & Learning Outcomes

Key Concept: SMSFs are regulated by the SIS Act and ITAA 97, and must comply with the sole purpose test to receive concessional tax treatment.

Understanding SMSFs requires knowledge of how different legislation interacts and how regulators interpret the law. The two key Acts are:

SIS Act 1993

Governs prudential management and supervision of superannuation funds

ITAA 97

Provides the tax regime applicable to all superannuation funds

Learning Outcomes

After completing this module, you should be able to:

  • Explain significant events in the history of superannuation in Australia
  • Identify key legislation governing SMSFs
  • Define an SMSF according to SIS Act section 17A
  • Discuss penalties for breaches of the SIS Act
  • Evaluate advantages and disadvantages of SMSFs

History of Superannuation in Australia

1909

Introduction of the Age Pension

1983

Taxation of superannuation benefits introduced

1986

Compulsory superannuation introduced into industrial awards

1992

Superannuation Guarantee (SG) introduced

1994

SIS Act introduced (effective 1 July 1994)

1999

SMSFs defined in SIS Act, regulation transferred to ATO

2007

'Better Super' reforms introduced

2017

Transfer Balance Cap ($1.6M) introduced

2021

SMSF member limit increased from 4 to 6

Note: The SG rate is gradually increasing to reach 12% by 1 July 2025. As of 2024/25, it's 11.5%.

Case Study: SMSF Growth

In June 1996, there were 100,447 small superannuation funds with total assets of $28 billion. By June 2023, there were around 610,000 SMSFs with over 1.1 million members, holding around $876 billion (approximately 25%) of total superannuation assets.

Legislative Framework

SMSFs are trusts governed by:

  • General trust law (common law)
  • The fund's trust deed
  • Statutory legislation (primarily SIS Act and ITAA 97)

Key Concept: Four characteristics determine whether a trust exists: trustees, trust property, beneficiaries, and trustee obligations.

Other Relevant Legislation

  • Corporations Act 2001
  • Superannuation Guarantee Act 1992
  • Family Law Act 1975
  • Social Security Act 1991
  • Trustee Acts (state-based)

Regulators & Dispute Resolution

Body Role
ATO Primary regulator of SMSFs
APRA Regulator of large funds and SAFs
ASIC Regulates financial services and auditors
AFCA Dispute resolution for financial complaints
Case Study: Licensing Requirements

Since 1 July 2016, accountants need to be licensed to provide SMSF advice. This includes recommending establishment or winding up of an SMSF, as these constitute financial product advice under the Corporations Act.
